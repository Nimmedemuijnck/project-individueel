What is their mission?

In a world facing the grave threat of climate change, an extraordinary organization known as Climate Cleanup emerged with a mission that was nothing short of astonishing: to remove 1500 gigatons of CO2 by harnessing the power of nature.

Their ambitious goal was to expand nature-centric businesses and create an emerging sector of climate solutions known as NatureTech. This sector would be the beating heart of regenerative efforts and lay the foundation for a novel, circular economy based on the concept of the doughnut.

By 2050, the visionaries behind Climate Cleanup envisioned a world where humanity and nature would thrive in perfect harmony on a living, breathing planet. A sector of Climate Cleanup companies toiled tirelessly on the colossal task of sequestering 1500 gigatons of CO2, a critical step in restoring the planet's carbon balance and stabilizing the climate to a healthy state.

The linchpin of this grand vision was nature-based solutions, which not only provided sustenance and meaningful employment but also breathed new life into neglected natural ecosystems. This marked the dawn of the doughnut-based New Nature Economy, a framework that not only helped avert the climate crisis but also rejuvenated the Earth.

Across the globe, nature began to recover in sync with humanity. People started to recognize the profound connection between themselves, all other forms of life, and the natural world. They became stewards of restoration, not only for our planet but also for their own species.

At the core of this transformation was a shift in our economic paradigm, moving away from an extractive approach to a regenerative one. Climate Cleanup spearheaded the Climate Cleanup sector, a pivotal component of the broader regenerative doughnut economy. The doughnut concept served as their compass, guiding them through the uncharted territory of a decentralized, nature-centric economy.

By 2025, Climate Cleanup had laid the groundwork for the Climate Cleanup sector through close collaboration between the Climate Cleanup Foundation, visionary entrepreneurs, and a multitude of stakeholders, including governments, financial institutions, scientists, and NGOs. Together, they developed holistic business models and carbon accounting methods rooted in the principles of the doughnut economics.

The theory of change was simple yet profound: to alter the world, you must construct a new model that renders the existing one obsolete. Climate Cleanup aimed to cultivate a new sector of Climate Cleanup entrepreneurs, ushering in a regenerative New Nature Economy capable of restoring nature and the carbon balance. This new economy was poised to gradually replace the destructive fossil fuel-driven and GDP-centric economic system.

The heart of their operational process involved five iterative steps: identifying climate cleanup initiatives, entrepreneurs, and businesses; comprehending their requirements; providing the conditions for success; assessing progress and returning to the starting point.

The theory of change was underpinned by the belief that innovative social entrepreneurs, those who took risks and created new opportunities, could circumvent the conservative influences of established interests, and lead this transformative endeavor. It was a systemic change theory that offered a glimmer of hope in the face of an urgent climate crisis.

With a collective focus on distributed growth, Climate Cleanup believed that restoring ecosystems required recognizing their intrinsic diversity. Through a decentralized approach, they aspired to construct and expand an entirely new sector, enabling numerous small initiatives to coalesce into a substantial impact.

Their journey was characterized by supporting innovative entrepreneurs, sharing knowledge about nature-based solutions, attracting capital, fostering ecosystems of partners, and advocating for supportive policies, as well as discontinuing public support for industries that obstructed progress. Their ultimate goal was to create a world where humanity and nature flourished in harmony, thereby fulfilling their mission of restoring the planet's carbon balance and healing the Earth.
